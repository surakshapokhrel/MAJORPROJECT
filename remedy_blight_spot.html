Remedies for Northern Blight

  • Plant the Right Corn: Choose corn types that are naturally resistant to northern blight. They’re designed to handle this disease better.
  • Change Where You Plant: Don’t plant corn in the same spot every year. Rotate with crops like beans or wheat to stop the disease from building up.
  • Clean Up After Harvest: Remove or bury leftover corn plants after harvesting. This prevents the disease from surviving until the next season.
  • Spray Early: If you see signs of blight, use fungicide sprays (like azoxystrobin) quickly to control the spread.
  • Give Plants Space: Don’t overcrowd your plants. Good airflow keeps the leaves dry and makes it harder for the disease to spread.
