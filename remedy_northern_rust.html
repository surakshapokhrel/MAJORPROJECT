Remedies for Southern Blight

  • Fix Wet Spots: Make sure the soil drains well and avoid overwatering. Southern blight thrives in soggy conditions.
  • Remove Sick Plants: Pull out and destroy infected plants right away to stop the disease from spreading to healthy ones.
  • Use Fungicides: Apply fungicides like flutolanil or penthiopyrad at the base of the plants to control the fungus.
  • Raise Your Plants: Plant corn in raised beds to keep the soil dry and improve aeration, which discourages southern blight.
  • Space Them Out: Give your plants enough room for airflow. This reduces humidity and makes it harder for blight to spread.
